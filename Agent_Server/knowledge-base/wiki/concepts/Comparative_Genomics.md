# Comparative Genomics

**Comparative Genomics** is the study of the relationship between the genomic features of different species. By comparing the DNA, RNA, or protein sequences of different organisms, researchers can identify conserved regions, evolutionary diverges, and the functions of specific genes.

## Key Techniques
- **Ortholog Identification**: Finding genes in different species that evolved from a common ancestral gene.
- **Phylogenetic Analysis**: Constructing evolutionary trees to estimate divergence times and common ancestors.
- **Synteny Analysis**: Studying the conservation of gene order on chromosomes.

## Application in this Study
Comparative genomics was used to analyze the evolutionary relationship between *Macrobrachium rosenbergii*, *M. nipponense*, and several Penaeidae species. This revealed that *M. rosenbergii* and *M. nipponense* shared a common ancestor ~94 million years ago and showed differences in the diversity of the CHH hormone family compared to Penaeidae.
