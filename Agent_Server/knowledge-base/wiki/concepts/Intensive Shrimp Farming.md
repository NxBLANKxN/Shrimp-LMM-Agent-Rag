# Intensive Shrimp Farming (集約式養殖)

**Definition**: Intensive shrimp farming is a high-density production system characterized by high stocking densities, the use of high-protein formulated feeds, and active management of the aquatic environment (aeration, water exchange, and health monitoring) to maximize yield per unit area.

## Characteristics
- **High Stocking Density**: Significantly more shrimp per square meter compared to extensive or semi-intensive systems.
- **Controlled Inputs**: Reliance on commercial feeds rather than natural pond productivity.
- **Environmental Control**: Heavy use of water wheels (aerators) to maintain dissolved oxygen and water exchange to manage waste.
- **Higher Risk**: More susceptible to rapid water quality crashes and the explosive spread of viral diseases (e.g., WSSV).

## Challenges
- **Water Quality Degradation**: Accumulation of nitrogenous wastes (Ammonia, Nitrite) due to high feeding rates and excretion.
- **Disease Management**: High density increases stress and the transmission rate of pathogens.
- **Resource Intensity**: Requires significant energy for aeration and high-quality feed inputs.

## Optimization Techniques
- **Partial Harvesting**: To manage biomass and reduce risks.
- **Biofloc Technology**: Using beneficial bacteria to process waste into feed.
- **Strict Biosecurity**: To prevent the introduction of viruses.
