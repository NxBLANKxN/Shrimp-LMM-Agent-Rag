# Antimicrobial Peptides

**Antimicrobial Peptides (AMPs)** are short-chain peptides that act as key components of the innate immune system in many organisms, including crustaceans. They exhibit broad-spectrum activity against bacteria, fungi, and viruses.

## Characteristics
- Typically small size (often < 50 amino acids).
- Often contain a **signal peptide** to guide them to the cell surface or for secretion.
- Frequently possess cationic and amphipathic properties, allowing them to interact with microbial membranes.

## Examples in Crustaceans
Common types of AMPs in crustaceans include:
- **Crustins**
- **Histones** (e.g., Histone H2A)
- **Kazal-type inhibitors**
- **Lectins** (e.g., Techylectin)

## Application in this Study
The study identified 13 validated AMPs in *Macrobrachium rosenbergii* using the InverPep database and AI4AMP platform, highlighting the species' innate immune capabilities.
