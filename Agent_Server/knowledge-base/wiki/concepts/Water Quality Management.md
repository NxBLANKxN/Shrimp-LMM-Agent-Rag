# Water Quality Management (水質管理)

**Definition**: The process of monitoring and controlling the physical, chemical, and biological parameters of water in an aquaculture system to provide an optimal environment for the growth and health of the cultivated species.

## Key Parameters in Shrimp Farming
- **Dissolved Oxygen (DO)**: Critical for survival; usually maintained above 4.0 mg/L. Low DO causes stress and increases disease susceptibility.
- **pH**: Ideally maintained between 7.8 and 8.5. Rapid fluctuations or extreme values can be lethal.
- **Ammonia-N (TAN)**: A toxic byproduct of protein metabolism. Un-ionized ammonia ($\text{NH}_3$) is the most toxic form.
- **Nitrite ($\text{NO}_2^-$)**: An intermediate in the nitrification process; toxic at high concentrations, though less so in seawater than in freshwater.
- **Temperature**: Influences metabolism, growth rate, and oxygen solubility.
- **Salinity**: Affects osmoregulation; different species have different optimal ranges.

## Management Tools and Methods
- **Aeration**: Water wheels are used to increase DO and promote vertical mixing.
- **Water Exchange**: Replacing polluted water with clean seawater to dilute toxins.
- **Probiotics/Enzymes**: Adding beneficial bacteria to accelerate the breakdown of organic matter and ammonia.
- **Liming**: Adding lime to stabilize pH.

## Relationship with Biomass
As biomass increases in intensive systems, the demand for oxygen increases and the production of waste (Ammonia) rises, making active management essential to prevent system collapse.
