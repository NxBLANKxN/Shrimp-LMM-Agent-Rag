# Carrying Capacity (生產載量)

**Definition**: In aquaculture, carrying capacity refers to the maximum biomass of a species that a specific water body or pond system can support sustainably without causing detrimental changes to the water quality or inhibiting the growth and survival of the organisms.

## Factors Influencing Carrying Capacity
- **Dissolved Oxygen (DO)**: The amount of oxygen available limits the maximum biomass; higher aeration (e.g., via water wheels) can increase carrying capacity.
- **Nutrient Loading**: The accumulation of ammonia, nitrite, and organic matter from feed and excretion can lower the effective carrying capacity.
- **Water Exchange Rate**: Higher rates of water exchange can remove wastes and replenish oxygen, increasing the capacity.
- **Stocking Density**: Overstocking beyond the carrying capacity leads to slow growth, increased disease susceptibility, and higher mortality.

## Management Strategies
To maintain or optimize carrying capacity, farmers use:
- **Partial Harvesting**: Removing biomass periodically to stay within the pond's limits.
- **Aeration**: Increasing oxygen supply to support higher biomass.
- **Water Purification**: Using probiotics or chemical treatments to manage nitrogenous wastes.
