# Crustacean Hyperglycemic Hormone

**Crustacean Hyperglycemic Hormone (CHH)** is a family of neuropeptides produced in the X-organ-sinus gland complex of crustaceans. These hormones play a central role in regulating glucose metabolism, osmoregulation, and other physiological processes.

## CHH Family Classification
The family is generally divided into two types based on sequence similarity:
- **Type I (CHH/ITP)**: Includes CHH and Ion Transport Peptides (ITP). They are primarily involved in increasing blood glucose levels.
- **Type II (MIH/GIH/VIH)**: Includes Molt-Inhibiting Hormone (MIH), Gonad-Inhibiting Hormone (GIH), and Vitellogenesis-Inhibiting Hormone (VIH). They regulate molting and reproduction.

## Physiological Role
Removal of the eyestalks (where the X-organ-sinus gland is located) reduces the levels of these hormones, which can accelerate sexual maturation or molting in aquaculture practices.

## Application in this Study
The study identified 8 CHH-related proteins in *Macrobrachium rosenbergii* and found that the Palaemonidae family has lower CHH diversity and quantity compared to the Penaeidae family.
