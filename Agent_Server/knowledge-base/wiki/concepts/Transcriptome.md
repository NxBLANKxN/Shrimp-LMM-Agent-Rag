# Transcriptome

The **transcriptome** is the complete set of RNA transcripts that are produced by the genome of an organism. Unlike the genome, which is relatively static, the transcriptome is dynamic and varies depending on the cell type, developmental stage, and environmental conditions.

## Importance in Research
Analyzing the transcriptome allows researchers to:
- Identify which genes are actively expressed under specific conditions.
- Study alternative splicing and the resulting protein isoforms.
- Quantify gene expression levels to understand regulatory mechanisms.

## Application in this Study
In the study of *Macrobrachium rosenbergii*, the transcriptome was assembled from RNA-seq data to identify unique genes and proteins, providing a foundation for functional annotation and comparative genomics.
