# Good Aquaculture Practices (GAP)

**Definition:** A set of standards and certification schemes (e.g., BAP - Best Aquaculture Practices) that define responsible farm management practices to ensure environmental sustainability, food safety, and social responsibility.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- **Resource Efficiency**: Optimum stocking densities and feeding regimes to reduce waste.
- **Biosecurity**: Strict protocols to prevent disease outbreaks.
- **Environmental Stewardship**: Reducing pollution and preventing mangrove destruction.
- **Market Access**: Certification allows farmers to sell into high-value international markets.

**Challenges:**
- High cost of certification for small-scale farmers.
- Risk of fraud to meet strict international standards.
