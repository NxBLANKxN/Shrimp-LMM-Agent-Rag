# Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR)

**Definition:** A measure of an animal's efficiency in converting feed mass into increased body mass. It is calculated as the total amount of feed given divided by the weight gain of the animal.

**Importance in Aquaculture:**
- A lower FCR indicates higher efficiency and lower environmental cost (less waste, less raw material needed).
- High FCRs in small-scale farms are often seen as technical inefficiencies.
- Improving FCR is a key strategy for "streamlining" shrimp aquaculture to increase profitability and sustainability.
