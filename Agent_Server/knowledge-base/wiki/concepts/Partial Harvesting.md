# Partial Harvesting (間捕)

**Definition**: Partial harvesting is a management technique in aquaculture where individuals that have reached a target market size are selectively harvested throughout the production cycle, while smaller individuals are left in the pond to continue growing.

## Key Benefits
- **Biomass Control**: Reduces the overall biomass in the pond, preventing the system from exceeding its carrying capacity.
- **Water Quality Maintenance**: Lower biomass leads to reduced excretion and lower oxygen demand, helping to stabilize pH and dissolved oxygen levels.
- **Growth Enhancement**: Reduces competition for food and space, allowing smaller shrimp to grow faster.
- **Risk Reduction**: Lowers the risk of mass mortality associated with overcrowding and water quality crashes.
- **Economic Efficiency**: Allows for a steady stream of income and the ability to sell shrimp when market prices are highest.

## Application in Shrimp Farming
In intensive shrimp ponds, traditional single-harvest methods often lead to late-stage water quality degradation. Partial harvesting (e.g., using traps) has been shown to increase total yield per hectare. For example, in Kuruma shrimp (*Penaeus japonicus*) ponds, this method has helped achieve yields up to 28 tons/ha.
