# Macrobrachium nipponense

***Macrobrachium nipponense***, known as the Oriental river prawn, is a freshwater prawn species used in comparative genomic studies.

## Key Facts
- **Family**: Palaemonidae.
- **Genome**: Has a larger genome (approx. 4.5 Gb) and 52 pairs of chromosomes.
- **Evolution**: Evidence suggests a whole-genome duplication (WGD) event occurred approximately 109 million years ago.

## Comparison with M. rosenbergii
*M. nipponense* and *M. rosenbergii* shared a common ancestor approximately 94 million years ago. Both exhibit lower diversity in the Crustacean Hyperglycemic Hormone (CHH) family compared to species in the Penaeidae family.
