# Transcriptome assembling and multi-omics analysis of Macrobrachium rosenbergii

**Date:** June 2022
**Author:** Chih-Hsin Kao (高寘昕)
**Advisor:** Chung-Yen Lin, Ph. D. (林仲彥 博士)
**Source:** Master Thesis, National Taiwan University

## Key Findings
- **High-Quality Transcriptome**: Assembled a comprehensive transcriptome for *Macrobrachium rosenbergii* using 20 RNA-seq samples from various life stages and infection conditions (MrNV, *Vibrio harveyi*, and Whispovirus).
- **Assembly Metrics**: The final assembly consisted of approximately 209,351 clustered contigs with a BUSCO completeness of 96.5% (arthropoda_odb10).
- **Protein Prediction**: Predicted 154,901 protein sequences (length > 30 aa).
- **AMP Identification**: Identified 13 validated antimicrobial peptides (AMPs) including crustin, Histone H2A, CgUbiquitin, kazal2, Techylectin-5B, and MrH4, and 232 potential AMPs containing signal peptides.
- **CHH Family Analysis**: Identified 8 members of the Crustacean Hyperglycemic Hormone (CHH) family in *M. rosenbergii*, noting that the number and diversity of CHHs in Palaemonidae are lower than in Penaeidae.
- **Phylogeny**: Estimated that the common ancestor of *M. rosenbergii* and *M. nipponense* appeared approximately 94 million years ago.
- **MOLAS Database**: Developed the Multi-Omics online Analysis System (MOLAS), a web database integrating genomic, transcriptomic, and proteomic data for *M. rosenbergii* and other common Asian shrimp species (*L. vannamei*, *P. monodon*, *F. chinensis*, *M. japonicus*, *M. nipponense*).

## Full Summary
This research aims to enhance the genomic resources available for the giant river prawn (*Macrobrachium rosenbergii*), a species of significant economic importance in aquaculture. Due to the lack of a complete genome, the study employed a *de novo* transcriptome assembly strategy. By integrating 20 RNA-seq datasets from the NCBI Sequence Read Archive (SRA)—covering different life stages (post-larvae, juvenile, adult) and various infection states (MrNV, *Vibrio harveyi*, and Whispovirus)—the author constructed a high-quality transcriptome.

The assembly process involved quality filtering with Trimomatic, assembly via Trinity, and redundancy reduction using CD-HIT. The resulting transcriptome was validated using BUSCO, achieving 96.5% completeness for arthropods. Functional annotation was performed using SignalP, TMHMM, Pfam, NR, and KEGG databases, providing insights into the protein composition and metabolic pathways of the species.

A key focus was the identification of antimicrobial peptides (AMPs), which are critical for the shrimp's immune response. Using the AI4AMP prediction platform and the InverPep database, the study identified several validated AMP sequences. Additionally, the research explored the evolution of the Crustacean Hyperglycemic Hormone (CHH) family, discovering that *M. rosenbergii* has a more limited set of CHH proteins compared to species in the Penaeidae family, despite potential whole-genome duplication events in some Palaemonidae species.

Finally, the author integrated these findings into the Multi-Omics online Analysis System (MOLAS), providing a user-friendly graphical interface for researchers to analyze gene expression and genomic data across multiple Asian shrimp species. This resource is intended to support future studies in basic physiology, disease control, and precision breeding.
